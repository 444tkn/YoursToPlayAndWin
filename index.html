Algebraic Notation
What is algebraic notation?
It is one of the codes in which a chess game can be translated. Since the 1980s, it has become the most common way to record and describe the moves on the chessmat. Before then, descriptive notation was commonly used.

The files (vertical columns) are labeled from a to h, whereas the ranks (horizontal rows) are numbered from 1 to 8. Each piece has its own uppercase character code, except for the pawn. The piece's name precedes the destination square. If a capture happens, it shall be indicated with an “x” inserted inbetween the piece and the destination square.

Special moves have their own specific notations. An en passant adds the characters “e.p.” at the end of the move. A promotion appends an “=” and the identifier of the new piece after the destination square and so on. You will find below the algebraic notation's codes and grammar.

Story
What's the story?
Named after a village on the Isle of Lewis, the Crowlista typeface is adapted to the game of chess with the Mono version and styles. With a hint in modern mechanism coming from the slab serif genre, the mono proportions adapt the glyphs to the modular elements of the game. The rounded edges give an organic quality which refers to human's inherent quality: thinking.

Duchamp said that “chess pieces are the block alphabet which shapes thoughts.” Well, The block alphabet does that itself as well. However, it is only once the symbols, whether they are pieces or glyphs, are activated that they become meaningful. This process happens once they give an ideographic representation of an abstract image, a poetic idea. It is now ours to use these symbols, in order to share and learn, to describe and picture, to write and to read.

It complements the flourishing 444 Squadra font catalogue, alongside Crowlista's proportionnal version, Arsens, Ella, Margaux and Snurfer. The studio is a two-people job, with Antonin Maudry and Thomas K. Neyroud.

Yours to Play and Win and the Crowlista Mono family are part of Thomas K. Neyroud's diploma project at ECAL, 2023.
